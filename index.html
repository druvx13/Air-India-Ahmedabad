Executive Summary

Catastrophic Accident

Air India Flight AI171 crashed shortly after takeoff into a residential area, resulting in significant loss of life both on board and on the ground.

On June 12, 2025, Air India Flight AI171, a Boeing 787-8 Dreamliner en route from Ahmedabad (AMD) to London Gatwick (LGW), experienced a catastrophic accident shortly after takeoff. The aircraft, carrying 242 individuals (232 passengers and 10 crew), crashed into a densely populated residential area known as Meghani Nagar.

A substantial fuel load for the long-haul flight contributed to a massive post-impact fire, resulting in significant casualties among those on board and on the ground. This marks the first fatal crash involving a Boeing 787 Dreamliner globally.

Cause of the accident is currently under rigorous investigation by India's Aircraft Accident Investigation Bureau (AAIB) and DGCA, with international cooperation from UK AAIB and US NTSB.

Casualties

  • No survivors reported from onboard the aircraft
  • Ground casualties at doctors' hostel
  • At least 204 bodies recovered from site

Investigation Status

  • Black boxes recovered and being analyzed
  • International cooperation with UK AAIB & US NTSB
  • Final report anticipated in 12-18 months

Investigation Framework

Indian Authorities

  • AAIB: Primary investigation agency under Ministry of Civil Aviation
  • DGCA: India's aviation safety regulator coordinating response
  • Top officials dispatched immediately to crash site

International Cooperation

  • UK AAIB: Involved due to British citizens onboard
  • US NTSB: Involved as aircraft manufactured in US
  • Governed by ICAO Annex 13 protocols

Black Box Recovery and Analysis

Cockpit Voice Recorder

Captures audio from the cockpit

Flight Data Recorder

Records hundreds of flight parameters

Both the Cockpit Voice Recorder (CVR) and Flight Data Recorder (FDR) were recovered from the crash site within 24 hours. Preliminary analysis shows:

  • Complete flight data up to impact
  • Multiple audible cockpit alarms prior to crash
  • Fuel system anomalies detected

Full decoding of the recorders is being conducted at the AAIB's laboratory with international experts present.

Immediate Safety Recommendations

Aircraft Operations

  • Enhanced pre-flight fuel system checks
  • Revised takeoff emergency procedures
  • Crew resource management protocols

Airport Infrastructure

  • Review of obstacle clearance zones
  • Emergency response drills enhancement
  • Urban development restrictions near airports

These are interim recommendations pending final investigation. Airlines and authorities are advised to implement immediately.
